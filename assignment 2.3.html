Types of Cancer:

Cancer manifests in various forms, each with distinct characteristics and treatment approaches

  1. Carcinomas:

    These cancers originate in epithelial cells, covering external and internal body surfaces. Adenocarcinomas and squamous cell carcinomas are common subtypes, often found in organs like the lungs, breast, and skin.

  2. Sarcomas:

    Developing in connective tissues like bones and muscles, sarcomas are less common but can be aggressive. Osteosarcoma, arising in bone tissue, is an example of a sarcoma.

  3. Lymphomas:

    Affecting the lymphatic system, lymphomas can be Hodgkin or non-Hodgkin. They involve the abnormal growth of cells in lymph nodes, spleen, and other immune system components.

  4. Leukemias:

    These cancers impact blood and bone marrow, leading to an overproduction of abnormal blood cells. Acute and chronic forms exist, depending on the speed of progression and cell type involved.

  5. Central nervous system cancers

    Tumors in the brain and spinal cord can be primary or result from the spread of cancer from other parts of the body.
